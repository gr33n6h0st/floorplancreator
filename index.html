B1. Technical Concepts & A1. Development

01 / Technical Specifications & Usability

Floorplancreator.net functions as a lightweight architectural suite[cite: 6]. To satisfy Overall Expectation B1 regarding technical concepts[cite: 17], the tool uses a specific workflow:

  • 01. The Smart-Snap System: Users drag walls, windows, and doors onto the canvas. The software automatically heals joints and aligns elements to maintain structural accuracy[cite: 7, 8].
  • 02. Input Rigor: Unlike simple sketch apps, this tool allows for exact metric or imperial dimension inputs to create scalable technical drawings[cite: 9].
  • 03. Symbol Literacy: Students must utilize standard architectural symbols, such as door swings and electrical icons, for professional model verification[cite: 18].

Instant 3D Visualization

The tool's most sophisticated feature is the one-click 2D-to-3D transformation[cite: 10]. This provides students with an immediate feedback engine to verify sightlines and ergonomics.

This aligns with Overall Expectation A1, as it allows students to "fail fast" and iterate their designs multiple times in a single period without "Creation Fatigue"[cite: 11, 14, 32].

B. Accessibility Retrofit (AODA)

Students import JPEGs of old layouts and renovate them to meet AODA standards, specifically the 1500mm turning radius[cite: 24, 25]. The 3D mode is used to test wheelchair maneuverability[cite: 26].

C. Collaborative BIM Workflow

Mimicking modern Building Information Modeling (BIM), students use "Project Sharing" to work on a single file simultaneously, with different students optimizing specific zones (e.g., kitchen triangle)[cite: 27, 28, 30].
