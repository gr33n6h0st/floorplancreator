01 / Technical Workflow & Usability

Fig 1.1 / Interface

To satisfy Overall Expectation B1 regarding technical concepts, the tool utilizes a specific three-step workflow:

The Smart-Snap System

Automatic joint healing and alignment ensure structural accuracy during the drafting phase, allowing for professional-grade results.

Input Rigor

Support for exact metric dimensioning moves students away from sketching and toward professional technical modeling standards.

Symbol Literacy

Students utilize standard architectural symbols (door swings, electrical icons) for industry-standard model verification.

02 / Feedback Engine

Fig 2.1 / 3D Verification

Instant 3D Visualization

The "Killer Feature" is the one-click 2D-to-3D transformation. This allows for instant ergonomic verification and sightline analysis. By removing the creation fatigue of manual drafting, students focus on Expectation A1: evaluating and refining solutions through rapid prototyping.

03 / Pedagogical Rationale & Context

Scenario A: Tiny Home Challenge

Math Integration
Fig 3.1 / Area Calc

Students manage spatial budgets under 37mÂ² via Live Area Calculation. This provides immediate mathematical scaffolding, allowing students to see the impact of their design choices on square footage in real-time.

Scenario B: Accessibility Retrofit

AODA Standards
Fig 3.2 / Compliance

Students verify wheelchair turning radii (1500mm) within the software to meet professional Ontario AODA accessibility standards. Using 3D mode, they can "walk through" the space to test maneuverability and build empathy for different users.

Scenario C: Collaborative Industry Workflow

Industry BIM
Fig 3.3 / Collaboration

Mimicking Building Information Modeling (BIM) workflows, students use cloud-based sharing to collaborate on a single file. This encourages professional communication and the optimization of complex zones like the kitchen work triangle.
